Case Study: UPI / Fraud Involving Online Payment

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the hottest cybercrimes that is rapidly increasing in India infecting app users such as Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, etc. It involves deceiving people into authorizing illegal pure fraudulent transactions unknowingly.

A typical case begins when a victim receives the call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official, delivery or buyer. The fraudster induces a sense of urgency and sends a "collect request" in the form of a UPI app. The victim is instructed to enter their UPI PIN; the false reason for entering the PIN is to receive money. Once the PIN is entered, the money is immediately received by the scammer's account from the victim's account.

Students and young adults are popular targets because of their frequent use of digital payment applications and general lack of awareness about fraud schemes. The consequences are financial loss, emotional sufferings, and at times, the personal data may get compromised as well.

For instance, various such incidents reported from India reveal victims of losing thousands of rupees within a few minutes because of fake collect requests. With UPI transactions being instant, it is hard to get any recovery if not reported immediately.

This case brings out the importance of awareness, caution, and the verification of all payment requests before authorization of transactions.
