Online safety prevention tips
Start by keeping your UPI PIN locked in silence. Someone asking? That’s a red flag already. Your OTP meant only for you - never share it out loud or online. Banking information stays private, always. Handing it over? Not wise under any condition. Each detail matters just as much on its own.
Here's something key to keep in mind. Making money never requires giving out your PIN. That detail stays yours alone. Never hand it over, under any setup. It plays no role in earning income. Always hold back that number. No legitimate path asks for it. Your security comes first, every time.
Finding a payment you do not recognize? Best to check it first before giving approval. A quick look could prevent bigger problems later on.
Watch out for odd links arriving by text, inbox, or chat messages. A strange message might carry hidden risks. Spotting them early helps avoid trouble later. Unexpected notes from unknown sources often lead nowhere safe. Think twice before tapping any unfamiliar web address.
Beware of fake versions when downloading payment tools like Google Pay or PhonePe. Stick to trusted places for installation. Unknown websites might offer harmful copies instead. Always confirm the source before any setup begins. Safety comes first with financial apps anywhere.
Enable app lock and two-factor authentication for financial applications.
Every now and then, take a look at your bank statements along with the list of transactions.
Avoid setting up any screen-sharing tools if you do not recognize them. Remote access programs can slip in unnoticed. Unfamiliar apps might start running without clear warnings. Stay cautious when something asks to connect to your display. Unknown software could take control quietly. Think twice before allowing access to your machine. Software that shows your screen may already be watching.
Hold off when a payment ask pops up out of nowhere. That sudden prompt? Best ignored straight away.
Beware of where your cash goes. Check the person’s name before hitting send. Money moves fast, so slow down a second. Wrong names mean lost funds. Confirm every time - it only takes a glance. That detail matters more than you think.
A single password should never repeat across sites. Each login needs something tough to guess instead.
